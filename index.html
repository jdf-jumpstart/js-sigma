Hover a chip to see how we pin down color

Where chips came from. Before 1963, "red" meant one thing to a designer and something else to whoever printed the job. Pantone gave every ink a number, and the argument ended.

Why we think this hard about color. Color theory is the part most practices skip. It decides whether your palette reads as clinical or calm, whether a green that looks right on a backlit phone still looks right on a printed intake form, and whether your text stays legible for a patient with low vision. We settle all of that before anything gets designed, then pin it to exact values so it can't drift later.

Web design

Your site has one job. Say what you treat, show what it costs, and let someone book without calling first to ask how any of it works. Most practice sites do none of that, which is why they get traffic and no patients.
